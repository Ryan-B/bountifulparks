Twin Hollows United: Rallying for Bountiful's Open Space

Contributed by John Pitt

The Bountiful City Council anticipated some level of opposition to a local developer's recent request for endorsement of a proposed public/private landswap. So they called for a public hearing to gauge community response to the idea. What they didn't anticipate was the formation of a determined neighborhood coalition called Twin Hollows United.

United indeed, in their opposition to development of the 156 acres of Forest Service property that has kept the hills above their homes open for hiking, horseback riding, and sunset watching for generations. The group did more than pack a record number of people into City Hall to speak against the proposal. They sent hundreds of well reasoned e-mails to City Officials, ignited followings on social media, organized themselves into various committees to find experts to back up their claims and verify their concerns. They even organized a neighborhood party to draw attention to the tremendous benefit of easy access to open space for Bountiful families.

Best of all, they provided a catalyst to bring a neighborhood together in defense of a vital public resource for Our Bountiful Community. The net effect of which was a letter of opposition from the City Council to the Forest Service in Washington D.C. and a commitment for review of open space policy by Bountiful officials...JSP
